THE ORIGIN OF CJP

During a Supreme Court hearing on 15 May 2026 regarding a contempt petition related to senior advocate designations and the use of fraudulent professional credentials, Chief Justice Surya Kant remarked:

“There are youngsters like cockroaches, who don't get any employment or have any place in the profession. Some of them become media, some of them become social media, RTI activists, and other activists, and they start attacking everyone.”

The following day, Abhijeet Dipke announced the launch of a platform for all the “cockroaches” out there, describing it as a voice for unemployed, chronically online, outspoken youth who were tired of being ignored.

The Cockroach Janta Party (CJP) website officially launched on 16 May under the tagline: “Voice of the Lazy & Unemployed.”

Artificial intelligence tools such as ChatGPT and Claude were used to build the website, manifesto, graphics, and digital campaign identity.

The movement spread rapidly across social media platforms and became one of the fastest-growing youth-driven digital campaigns in India.

Supporters organised online campaigns, offline protests, public discussions, awareness drives, and community activities across multiple Indian states.

Following the backlash, Chief Justice Surya Kant later clarified that his comments were directed specifically toward individuals using fake professional degrees and not unemployed youth in general.

“A political front of the youth, by the youth, for the youth — Secular, Socialist, Democratic, and Lazy.”

The movement states clearly that it is not interested in becoming a traditional political party controlled by career politicians. Instead, it positions itself as a digital-age public movement driven by satire, criticism, accountability, creativity, and youth participation.

“We are here to ask, loudly, repeatedly, in writing — where the money went.”
“Build a movement for young people who keep getting called lazy, chronically online, and cockroaches. The rest is satire.”

WHY JUSTICE NEEDED A NEW VOICE

For years, people watched systems fail silently. Electricity crises. Rising costs. Empty promises. Political drama without public progress.

The founders of CJP believed that true justice is not delivered in speeches — it is delivered through accountability, courage, and public action.

The movement began as a symbolic rebellion against corruption and negligence.

OUR MOTIVE

Cockroach Janta Party exists for one reason: To challenge silence.

CJP believes politics should not belong only to the powerful. It should belong to students, workers, creators, and ordinary citizens.

  • Speak against public issues
  • Empower youth voices
  • Expose corruption
  • Encourage political awareness
  • Build fearless public discussion

THE CJP FIVE-POINT AGENDA

1. Judicial Neutrality

CJP believes judicial independence must remain untouched by political influence.

2. Protect Every Vote

Democracy survives only when every citizen’s legitimate vote is protected.

3. Equal Representation

Women deserve equal participation in governance and leadership.

4. Independent Media

Democracy needs fearless journalism that questions power instead of serving it.

5. Anti-Defection Reform

Political defections weaken democracy and betray public trust.
